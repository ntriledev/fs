# FlirtSpot LED Billboard in Lagos — Possibilities Research

> Use case: anonymous **proximity-dating app**, targeting **slow-moving, tech-savvy pedestrians**.
> Date compiled: 2026-07-06 · Prices in NGN (Lagos OOH market, 2025–2026 sources).

---

## TL;DR

Lagos's traditional "best" LED spots (Falomo Bridge, Third Mainland, Alexander
Roundabout) are **wrong** for FlirtSpot — they're vehicle-traffic screens where
drivers can't scan a QR or stop. The real fit is:

1. **Mall / cinema digital screens** (dwell time, walking speed, phone-in-hand) — Lekki & Ikeja
2. **Yaba / "Yabacon Valley"** screens — Nigeria's tech hub, dense pedestrian tech crowd
3. **Lekki corridor** (Admiralty Way, Circle Mall) — young professionals, tech workers

Realistic all-in budget for a meaningful campaign: **₦2–6M/month**
(~$1,300–4,000 USD), shared-screen slots from ~₦160k/day.

---

## 1. The Lagos DOOH (Digital Out-of-Home) landscape

Lagos is **Africa's largest OOH ad market**. ~21M residents, brutal traffic =
massive captive eyeballs.

- Market dominated by **JCDecaux Nigeria, Alliance Media, Outdoors.ng,
  Primedia Outdoor**, plus local media owners.
- All outdoor ads are regulated by **LASAA** (Lagos State Signage &
  Advertisement Agency) + **APCON** (content vetting).
- A new **digital permit portal + QR permits** launched **May 2026** —
  structures not registered are now classified illegal and torn down.
- LED screens typically run **shared rotation** (your ad is one slot among
  ~6–10 brands looping every few minutes), sold **per day / per hour /
  per month**.

## 3. Locations that actually fit FlirtSpot (pedestrian + tech-savvy)

### 🥇 Yaba — "Yabacon Valley" (BEST conceptual fit)
Mainland suburb, Nigeria's tech hub. Home to **CcHub, MainOne fibre, TheNest
Innovation Park**, multiple universities + tech startups. Dense, young,
phone-native, *pedestrian* crowd around Herbert Macaulay Way / Yaba Tech.
This is the exact "tech-savvy + on-foot" persona. Inventory is smaller and
not always marketed online — ask agencies for Yaba/Akoka screens specifically.

### 🥈 Mall & cinema digital screens (BEST practical fit)
Pedestrian dwell time, walking pace, phones already out — ideal for a
**scannable QR + app-download** message.

- **Circle Mall, 5th Roundabout, Lekki** — ₦1.8M/mo, Shoprite + Filmhouse
  anchored, 14,400 exposure
- **Filmhouse IMAX, Durosimi Etti, Lekki** — ₦1.15M/mo, **interactive
  touchscreen + 24MP camera** (huge for a dating app — could run a live
  "radar" activation)
- **Ikeja City Mall** (Silverbird cinema, Allen/Alausa area) — premium
  retail, young affluent shoppers
- **Filmhouse cinemas** (13 cinemas / 6 states) — foyer screens, high dwell time

### 🥉 Lekki corridor (Admiralty Way, Chevron Drive)
"Young professionals, tech workers, affluent families" — priced **2–3×
mainland**, but audience matches. Screens *along* Lekki-Epe Expressway are
car-traffic; mall screens here are the pedestrian play.

### ❌ Skip for FlirtSpot
Falomo Bridge, Alexander Roundabout, Third Mainland, unipoles on expressways.
Built for **drivers stuck in traffic** — nobody will scan a QR or download an
anonymous dating app from a moving car. Also the most expensive slots in the
country.

## 4. Strategic recommendation for FlirtSpot

**Tier 1 — test & learn (1–2 months):** One mall/cinema screen in Lekki
(Filmhouse or Circle) + one in Ikeja. Budget ~₦2.5–4M all-in. Use the
**interactive screen's camera** to run a live "radar sweep" mirroring the
in-app radar — on-brand and impossible to ignore on foot.

**Tier 2 — own the tech crowd:** Add Yaba once inventory is sourced (ask
Optimum / Alternative Adverts to find it). Pair with **BRT bus branding**
(₦180k–300k/bus/mo, Primero blue = more affluent commuters) for citywide
mobile reach.

### Creative rules for a dating app on Lagos screens
- **APCON will vet content** — Nigeria's ad decency standards are
  conservative. Lead with "anonymous *social/spark* app," keep imagery
  clean, show the **18+** disclaimer visibly. Avoid anything that reads as
  explicit or you'll be rejected.
- **QR must be huge** — pedestrian screens mean ~3–8m viewing distance; QR
  needs to fill ≥15–20% of the frame to scan reliably.
- **Loop length ≤ 8 sec** — shared rotation gives short slots; one message,
  one QR, one CTA.

## 6. Compliance checklist (do NOT skip)

1. Screen structure registered in **LASAA Digital Directory Portal** (since
   May 2026, unregistered = illegal).
2. Operator holds valid **LASAA permit** (QR-verifiable via LASAA One App).
3. Submit creative to **APCON vetting** (mandatory, content/decency).
4. Obtain **LASAA display permit** for your specific campaign.
5. Keep all permits for the full campaign — forced removal otherwise.

## Bottom line

Very doable and within a startup budget. The **wrong** move is buying
Lagos's famous expensive bridge screens; the **right** move is
**Lekki/Ikeja mall & cinema digital screens + Yaba**, leaning into the
interactive-screen + camera angle that mirrors FlirtSpot's radar. Budget
**₦2–6M/month all-in** for a credible run, 1–2 months to start.
